# Vitacure: AI-Driven Queue Management System (Concept & Research)

# Overview

*Vitacure* is a conceptual AI-based queue management system designed to optimize patient flow in outpatient departments (OPDs) and emergency hospital operations. This research-based proposal explores how machine learning, predictive analytics, and real-time data processing can be leveraged to enhance hospital efficiency, reduce patient wait times, and improve emergency triage management.

Important Note: Vitacure is currently a research concept presented at the SEED Hackathon and Smart India Hackathon. No development or implementation has been carried out yet.

# Abstract

Modern healthcare systems struggle with inefficient patient queues, leading to overcrowding, long wait times, and resource mismanagement. Traditional queue management methods lack the intelligence to prioritize patients dynamically based on urgency. Vitacure proposes an AI-driven approach that optimizes patient flow, predicts peak hospital hours, and enhances emergency triage with real-time insights. This research aims to explore the feasibility of such a system and its potential impact on healthcare operations.

# Key Highlights of the Research

✅ Dynamic Queue Optimization: Conceptual AI-driven prioritization based on urgency, appointment status, and hospital capacity.

✅ Predictive Analytics: Proposed forecasting model for peak hospital hours and resource allocation.

✅ Real-Time Notification System: Theoretical approach to reducing wait times through digital alerts.

✅ Enhanced Emergency Triage: AI-powered triage proposal for streamlining emergency care.

# Future Potential & Development Process

While Vitacure remains a conceptual project, it has strong potential for real-world application. Below is a roadmap for turning this research into an actual system:

1️⃣ Phase 1: Research Expansion & Feasibility Study

Conduct deeper research on hospital workflow challenges.

Collaborate with healthcare professionals to refine the approach.

Analyze existing queue management systems for benchmarking.

2️⃣ Phase 2: Prototyping & AI Model Development

Develop a small-scale prototype for testing.

Train AI models using simulated or real hospital data (if available).

Implement a basic scheduling and queue prioritization system.

3️⃣ Phase 3: System Development & Testing

Build a web or mobile-based interface for queue management.

Develop real-time notification and scheduling features.

Conduct testing with healthcare professionals for feedback.

4️⃣ Phase 4: Deployment & Scaling

Deploy in a controlled hospital environment as a pilot project.

Gather real-world data and improve AI efficiency.

Scale up for multiple hospitals and refine emergency triage features.
